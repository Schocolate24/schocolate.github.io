Intro to The SGI and Nichiren Buddhism

What is SGI?

SGI stands for Soka Gakkai International which is a buddhist organization of Nichiren Buddhism with 12 million practitioners in 192 countries and territories based on the practice of chanting Nam-Myoho-Rengekyo. We practice this Buddhism to enable each person to become happy and ultimately, to establish peace in the entire world.

What do we do in this practice?

We chant daimoku,"Nam-Myoho-Rengekyo", and recite the sutra every morning and evening to "Gohonzon" which is an object of when you chant daimoku. Every once a month, we have district meetings and a general meeting every weekend. We used to do it in person but now we do every meetings on Zoom for this challenging time.

The history of the SGI

The first president of the Soka Gakkai, Tunesaburo Makiguch, who was an educator for elementary school students, published a book called "The system of the value-creating education" on November 18, 1930, when he was 59.

Nam-Myoho-Rengekyo

We chant "Nam-myoho-Rengekyo", which is the rythm of the universe, as a practice of this Buddhism. "Nam" means respectation, "Myo" means revitalization, "Ho" means the Mystic law, "Renge" means lotus, and finally, "Kyo" means the sutra.
